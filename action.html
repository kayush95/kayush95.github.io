Generative Adversarial Networks for Action Recognition in Images.

  • Guide: Prof. Pabitra Mitra
  • Visual recognition of human-object interactions in images is a prerequisite for generating image descriptions and retrieving images by sentences. A key bottleneck to recognition of such interactions is the categories that have very few training examples. In such cases, semantic knowledge can significantly improve recognition. A rare category might piggyback on a co-occurring interaction that has more training data thus resulting in an improved recognition. In this thesis, I seek to study whether GANs can be used in a semi-supervised setting for recognition of human-object interactions in images, which is an important subset of the larger problem of action recognition. We intend to delve deeper and extend the work to videos as well
