PCF-based models for Performance Enhancement of Double Channel 802.11n WLAN

  • Abstract: Wi-Fi enabled hand-held devices quickly occupied the consumer market as a result of the remarkable customer acceptance of IEEE 802.11 standard. But the widely-used con- tention based medium access mechanism is unable to meet the increasing traffic demand of todays home users. Especially, delay sensitive multimedia contents suffer heavily from quality degradation under high traffic load. To cope with increasing throughput requirements, and to take advantage of improvements in hardware performance, 802.11n has been introduced, which is modelled solely on the basis of DCF-based contention periods. However, the time synchronized medium access mechanism of 802.11 called PCF offers lower delay and loss, and holds true potential in supporting high traffic load. For design and planning of such networks, a thorough performance analysis of PCF based 802.11 WLANs is of profound importance. In this paper, we attempt to propose a modification to the double channel 802.11n architecture by introducing PCF-based Contention-Free Periods between the DCF-based contention cycles to allow the central AP to relieve the starving node.
